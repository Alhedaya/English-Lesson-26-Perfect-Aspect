Perfect aspect We use perfect aspect to look back from a specific time and talk about things up to that time or about things that are important at that time.
There are three main tenses in the perfect aspect:
present perfect · past perfect "also called pluperfect" · future perfect.

Present Perfect
The present perfect tense is formed using the present tense of the auxiliary verb "to have" have · has followed by the past participle of the main verb.
It is used to indicate actions or states that started in the past and continue into the present, or actions that have relevance to the present moment.

Examples:

• She has lived in Paris for ten years.
Action started in the past and continues into the present.
• I have finished my homework.
Action completed with relevance to the present.
Past Perfect "Pluperfect"
The past perfect tense is formed using the past tense of the auxiliary verb "to have" had followed by the past participle of the main verb.
It is used to indicate actions or states that were completed before another action or time in the past.

Examples:

• He had already left when I arrived.
Action completed before another action in the past.
• We had finished dinner before the guests arrived.
Action completed before a specific time in the past.
Future Perfect
The future perfect tense is formed using the future tense of the auxiliary verb "to have" will have followed by the past participle of the main verb.
It is used to indicate actions or states that will be completed before a specified time or future action.

Examples:

• By next year, she will have graduated from university. Action completed before a specified time in the future.
• By the time you arrive, I will have finished cooking dinner.
Action completed before a future action.

In summary
the perfect aspect in English grammar is used to express the completion or reaching of a certain point in time before another action or time frame. The present perfect • past perfect • future perfect tenses allow speakers and writers to convey nuanced information about the timing and completion of events or actions.
